Implementing the Greeter Role

Elevating G12 Front Counter Service
University of Sydney
Shared Service Centre
August 2025

Current Service Challenges

Why Change is Needed

  • Customer confusion about who to approach
  • Deep focus disruption affects service quality
    • Staff concentrating on complex work requests naturally become less responsive to visitors
    • Interruptions can cause errors in detailed tasks and require mental reset time
  • Follow-up responsibilities easily forgotten
    • Interview candidates and lost property visitors overlooked when staff return to focused work
    • No systematic tracking of who has been seated and for how long
  • Security protocol gaps during busy periods
  • Impact on our professional reputation
"These challenges create friction for both customers and staff"

Professional Services Review

Our Opportunity: Leading Front Counter Excellence in a Digital World

  • PSR recognizes professional staff as "valued mission partners"
  • Digital transformation increases importance of quality face-to-face service
  • As services become more automated, human interaction becomes premium experience
  • Front counter expertise becomes competitive advantage for University
  • Career progression for professionals who excel at human-centered service delivery
  • Operations Leadership emphasis on service accountability and performance measurement aligns with PSR excellence goals
"Digital capabilities enhance our service - they don't replace our expertise"

The Greeter Solution

"Traffic Controller" Model

  • Central positioning with active engagement
    • Sits centrally, stands to greet and direct visitors
    • Open body language and eye contact create welcoming environment
    • Same-level interaction reduces perceived power distance
  • Intelligent traffic direction based on workloads
  • Professional first-point contact eliminates confusion
  • Protects focused work whilst maintaining service quality
  • Systematic visitor follow-up with proper documentation
  • Operational Excellence: Lost property, mail/packages, PPE distribution, contractor management
  • End-of-day coordination: Supply checks, handover notes, facility maintenance
Research shows standing and eye contact increase approachability and trust in service environments

Professional Standards for G12 Front Counter

Creating Excellence Through Consistency

  • Communication: Work-focused coordination during busy periods
  • Device Usage: Personal phones away from customer areas - we're in a highly visible space where directors, deans, and senior staff regularly pass through
  • Work Focus: Professional attention during customer interactions demonstrates accountability
  • Team Coordination: Clear, respectful collaboration builds trust
  • Professional Discretion: Step away from desk area for personal calls, including work-related calls requiring privacy
"Consistent professional standards create the excellence our University community expects"

Enhanced Team Coordination

Working as Professionals

  • Morning Coordination: Brief role assignment discussions
  • Professional Communication: "Let's focus on this rush, we can catch up at break"
  • Workload Management: Awareness and mutual support
  • Cultural Sensitivity: We work in an international environment where smiling and friendly demeanour are universal languages
  • Team Development: New team dynamics can create friction - professional teams work through differences constructively
  • Conflict Resolution: Address misunderstandings directly and professionally
  • Positive Intent: When someone's communication seems different, assume good intentions
  • Professional Boundaries: Avoid commenting on colleagues' personal matters unless directly relevant
"Professional coordination ensures excellent service delivery"

Implementation Framework

One-Month Trial Starting September 2nd

  • Trial Period: One month with evaluation and feedback
  • Leadership Support: Raj (HDA) and myself as team leader providing guidance
  • Daily Flexibility: Role assignments based on preferences and development needs
  • Operational Principles: Central greeter, backup awareness, systematic visitor follow-up
  • Two-person adaptations: Maintain heightened greeting awareness while focused on tasks
"This trial demonstrates our commitment to front counter innovation"
